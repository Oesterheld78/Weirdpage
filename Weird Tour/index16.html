Top 5 Facts About Blue Whales

  • The blue whale (scientifically referred to as balaenoptera musculus) is a large marine mammal that is part of the baleen whale (mysticeti) suborder and is the largest animal alive growing to lengths of up to 100 ft. long and weighing as much as 150 tons or more
  • They mainly catch their food by diving, and descend to depths of approximately 500m.
  • Due to their large size these marine mammals are rarely ever attacked.
  • Despite being the largest animal on earth the blue whales primary diet consists of krill, a small aquatic life form that resembles tiny shrimp.
  • A blue whales calls can be heard several miles away and far below the oceans surface.
